Website Proposal by Cierra Larke

1.           Client and Topic (25-word minimum):

The website I will be creating will be for a (currently fictitious) business I plan to run once I retire from my current job. For my business, I will be the owner of a festivities venue. The website will allow potential clients to review photographs of the venue, make reservations, write reviews, etc.

 

2.          Development Process and Engagement (50-word minimum):

Because I am making the website for myself, I will conduct research on several wedding/birthday/company-gathering/celebration venues. I will survey my friends and family members on what services they would want the website for a festivities venue to provide. I imagine engaged-to-be-married people will be my primary customer base, so I will read about current trends in the world of weddings so that I can include new wedding-specific content on the page. This will convey to potential clients that we are up to date on these things and care as much about their special day as they do.

 

3.          Testing (50-word minimum):

Once I feel like I am close to launch-ready and have already personally tested my website, I will ask my friends to peruse the pages and perform in-depth inspections to verify functionality of all the links and content. I will include Chrome, Safari, Firefox and Duck Duck Go browsers to test my website.

 

4.         Description (100-word minimum):

My business will be called Classic Celebrations Events Center and the goal of the business is to provide an indoor venue, outdoor celebration/ceremony area, transportation, parking, and much more to clients in need of a place to conduct weddings, parties, funerals, etc.

The purpose of the website will be for customers to look at pictures of the event center, schedule tours of the venue, review services provided by the event staff, leave reviews, and get information to contact any additional vendors we are partnered with.

            The intended target audience is any adult involved in the planning of an event that requires our building or outdoor reception space. Emphasis will be placed on customers looking to use the space for weddings.

            Because the target audience is such a vast demographic, there will not be too much specificity placed on the content besides the color schemes, typography, and overall composition being that of class, elegance, and maturity.

            Unique content on the website made by me will be the text for services offered and relevant photographs taken and edited by me.

            I will get sample venue photos from other websites because I do not have an events center of my own yet.

            The websites content will probably need to be updated weekly because I plan to display when the venue is booked and has no availability for specific dates. Besides the availability, other content will most likely only need to be updated once a quarter.

 

5.          Growth and Maintenance (50-word minimum)

I think the website will continue to grow over time as trends in the industry change, but only minor things on the website will be affected (ie: adding fresh content). While developing the website, I can make sure to add clean code and organized CSS.  To maintain the website, I will need to make sure all my links are working and that there are no lags or speed issues. Weekly tasks I will need to run will be checking to make sure all the links work properly and all the pages and image load correctly. I will use security software monthly to make sure there are no issues with the page. I will make sure to check for spelling errors, typographical errors, and things of that nature quarterly every time I add or refresh the pages content.

 

6.         Organization (100-word minimum)

My website will be a minimalist style: I plan to have a large banner or slideshow directly beneath the navigation menu, following that, a paragraph or two that is just a quick blurb about our venues mission statement, and then below that, clickable images that lead to different sub-topic pages.

The subtopics the website will include will be different types of events the venue offers, for example, if the topic is even, subtopics would include wedding, social event, corporate event, etc.

Main pages on the website will be: Home, Events, About Us, Calendar, Gallery, Testimonials, and Contact Us.

 

 

 

7.          Security (50-word minimum)

The website will not include an e-commerce component. There will not be any content password-protected on my website because I do not intent to post any confidential or non-public features. I will not have a username or login option for users to create accounts on the page either because I do not think the venue will have a large number of frequent or repeat customers.

Measures I can take to secure the website are to use security plug-ins, make sure the webpage is up to date, and use SSL to encrypt data (email addresses, etc).

 

8.         Web Hosting (100-word minimum): Please note that you aren't required to actually register a domain name or sign up for a hosting plan.

When comparing and contrasting different web hosts, I will consider the prices, the storage amount allotted, the customer service support and technical service support hours, as well as the companys reputation.

SSL will be necessary for the website because I would like clients to submit a request form with their name, phone, number, email address, etc for inquires and will need to make sure the data is encrypted between the user and the website.

JavaScript will need to be supported by the web host for various JavaScript components I plan to use on my webpage or add on as an option in the future.

The domain ClassicCelebrations.com is available at a premium price. My reasoning behind selecting this domain name is that it is shorter than the full company name, so it will be easier for customers to remember and quicker to say when referring someone.

 

9.         Marketing (50-word minimum)

The website will be marketed a number of ways, but primarily through social media, search engine optimization, and paid advertisement. I plan to use platforms like YouTube, Pinterest, Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn to promote my business.

Search engine optimization elements such as using keywords and building links can be covered so far in the website planning and building process.
